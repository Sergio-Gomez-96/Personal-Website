Manufacturing

​Due to the limited manufacturing equipment available I felt the best method of manufacturing the drone was a combination of 3D printing and carbon fiber tubing. The majority of the structure was 3D printed using my Lotmaxx SC-10 printer. This printer had a slightly extended print area that allowed most of the drone to be printed at a time.

The material used was a light weight PLA from eSun that was specifically designed for applications like ours. 

The carbon fiber tubes we used were 25 mm, 8 mm, and 22 mm in diameter. The rear motor mounts connect to the fuselage via 25 mm carbon fiber tubes. The internal frame consists of 8 mm carbon fiber tubes that span through the wings. The front motor brackets utilize the same 25 mm carbon fiber tubes. The landing gear is 22 mm carbon fiber tubes.

 I printed the motor mounts, ESC mounts, and right angle carbon fiber tubing connection using a Markforged Mark II 3D printer. 

Electronics

​We kept the cost of electronics low by using the pixhawk 4 flight controller bundle from Holybro. This kit came with a battery management board that I soldered the wires of the ESCs to. I also needed to extend some wires and create some long wires for the servos in our prototype.
