Aerobee eVTOL Capstone Project

​The Aerobee is an eVTOL delivery drone that was chosen as my capstone project. I chose this project to challenge myself and improve my skills as a mechanical engineer. I also wanted to choose a project that involved aerodynamics since I am interested in that field as a career.

Project Background

Problem: Currently the most expensive part of the delivery process is the “last mile” which consists of delivering the products from the warehouse to the customer’s home. This is due to the labor cost of drivers, gas, traffic, and inefficient delivery routes.

Solution: Reducing the cost of the last mile delivery can be done using autonomous flying drones. The drones would reduce cost by significantly reducing the labor needed to deliver, utilize electric energy over gas, and deliver packages faster.

Design Requirements

Payload

​The maximum payload that the drone must be able to carry is 10 lbs.

Weather Tolerance

​The drone must be able to withstand 20 knot wind speeds.

Hybrid Drone

​The drone must be a Vertical Take-Off and Landing (VTOL) drone.

Range

​The drone must have the ability to travel within a 10 mile radius of the take-off position.

Battery Life

​The drone must be able to travel for an hour and have 20% energy left over for emergency cases.

A.I.

​The drone must be able to operate autonomously and have manual override controls.

Challenges

All project have there challenges and this project's challenges can be summed up in the following sections:
- COVID
- Aerodynamics
- Manufacturing
- Motor and Battery Selection
- Electronics

COVID

COVID hindered our abilities to collaborate during the design and prototyping phases. During the prototyping phase it was difficult to get everyone together for assembling the drone since some of us weren't in the area.

Aerodynamics

This was my first time designing a drone that needed wings to generate lift and this took a lot of time to learn. The calculations were difficult and I found myself dedicating many hours to this part of the design phase.

Manufacturing

Manufacturing the drone proved difficult as we wanted to laser cut the ribs that provide strength to the wings however, this wasn't an option. We ended up printing the supports with the wings however, it didn't always turn out as expected and we needed to reprint sections of the wings as a result

Motor and Battery Selection

Knowing the thrust needed was easy to calculate but finding a motor and propeller combo was the difficult part. Many of the motors weren't capable of providing the thrust we needed for our drone. I stumbled upon an article about the Wingcopter drone and the motors they use. I was able to find the manufacturer and this provided a database of useful motors, propeller, and ESC combinations.
The battery was difficult to calculate since we needed a large battery to support the flight time design requirement. When I finished calculating the battery life needed the battery was heavier than the allotted amount which meant we needed to trim the weight of the drone.

Electronics

Finding the electronics for the project was difficult since the system we chose was open source. This meant there were many iterations of the same thing with their own sensors etc. and this made it difficult to choose parts. When assembling the drone it was difficult to put together the electronics since the instructions weren't clear and I had to rely on online forums.

Lessons Learned

​Coming into my capstone project I had a decent amount of experience working on projects whether they were conceptual or hands on. As a result I was confident in my abilities to run this project as the lead engineer. Although this project was my idea, it was still my most challenging project since this is the first time I was experiencing the process of mechanical design and merging different disciplines such as electrical and aerospace engineering.

As a result, I am confident that I have learned a ton from this project. First, I finally experienced what it is like to set design requirements based on market data and compare current designs in the market.

Next, I experienced aerodynamic engineering for the first time and was able to understand the fundamentals of how an aerial vehicle is able to generate lift from wings. 

I was able to learn about lift equations, especially how wing span and wing area affect lift and efficiency of flight. I also learned how to select an airfoil based on the coefficient of lift needed. I also researched and was able to choose the ideal type of tail and the details of the tail based on the coefficient of the moment from multiple axis.

​Finally, I expanded my understanding of brushless motors and propellers and how they generate thrust. I had a good understanding before this project however, I was able to use that experience and choose some motor, propeller, and ESC combinations that suited our design. I was also able to understand how to choose a suitable battery based on power need calculations for altitude, hover, forward flight, and descent.
